<p><p>Automotive interior fasteners refer to the various components used to securely join different parts of a vehicle's interior. Threaded fasteners, such as screws and bolts, are commonly used to connect components by turning them into pre-drilled holes, creating a secure and adjustable connection. Non-threaded fasteners, on the other hand, do not have threads and are typically used for applications where disassembly is not required. These include clips, snaps, and adhesives, which provide a reliable and permanent connection method for automotive interiors.</p></p>
