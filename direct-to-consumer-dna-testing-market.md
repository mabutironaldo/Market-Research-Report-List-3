<p><p>The direct-to-consumer DNA testing market consists of various types, including diagnostic screening, prenatal testing, newborn screening, and pre-implantation diagnosis, as well as relationship testing. Diagnostic screening allows individuals to understand their genetic predispositions for certain diseases or conditions. Prenatal testing helps expectant parents assess the risk of genetic abnormalities in their unborn child. Newborn screening tests newborns for specific genetic disorders shortly after birth. Pre-implantation diagnosis is used in assisted reproductive techniques to determine potential genetic disorders before embryo implantation. Relationship testing helps individuals verify biological relationships with family members.</p></p>
