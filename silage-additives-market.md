<p><p>Silage additives can be divided into different types based on their functions and components. Inoculants are added to promote beneficial fermentation and improve silage quality. Organic acids help lower pH levels and inhibit spoilage organisms. Sugars act as a source of energy for lactic acid bacteria, aiding in fermentation. Enzymes break down plant fibers and improve digestibility. NPN nutrients provide additional protein for microbial growth. Each type of additive plays a specific role in enhancing the fermentation and preservation of silage.</p></p>
