<p><p>Demulsifiers are chemicals used to separate emulsions, which are mixtures of oil and water that do not naturally mix. There are two main types of demulsifiers: oil soluble and water soluble. Oil soluble demulsifiers work by breaking down the oil components of an emulsion, while water soluble demulsifiers break down the water components. Oil soluble demulsifiers are used in oil-based applications such as refining and drilling, while water soluble demulsifiers are used in water-based applications such as wastewater treatment and food processing.</p></p>
