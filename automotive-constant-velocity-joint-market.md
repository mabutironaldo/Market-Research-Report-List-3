<p><strong>The Automotive Constant Velocity Joint Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Outboard Joints</li><li>Inboard Joints</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automotive constant velocity joints (CVJs) are crucial components in vehicles, allowing the transfer of torque at a constant speed between the transmission and the wheels. There are two main types of CVJs: outboard joints and inboard joints. Outboard joints connect the CV axle shafts to the hub assemblies on the wheels, transmitting power to turn the wheels. In contrast, inboard joints connect the CV axle shafts to the transmission, enabling torque transmission from the transmission to the wheels. Both types play vital roles in ensuring smooth power delivery and efficient operation of the vehicle's drivetrain system.</p></p>
