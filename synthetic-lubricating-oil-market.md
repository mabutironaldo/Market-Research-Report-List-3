<p><p>Synthetic lubricating oil market types include organic esters, synthetic hydrocarbons, polyether, polysiloxane (silicone oil), containing oil, and phosphate. Organic esters are made from esterification of organic acids and alcohols. Synthetic hydrocarbons are derived from crude oil through various refining processes. Polyether oils are produced from the reaction of epoxides with alcohols. Polysiloxane oils are silicone-based lubricants offering high thermal stability. Containing oils are blends of various synthetic base oils and additives. Phosphate-based lubricants offer excellent anti-wear and extreme pressure properties.</p></p>
