<p><ul><li>Four-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines</li><li>Two-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the Dual-Fuel Marine Engines market, there are two main types: Four-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines and Two-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines. Four-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines are designed with a separate fuel injection system for each cylinder, allowing for efficient combustion of both liquid fuel (such as diesel) and gaseous fuel (such as natural gas). Meanwhile, Two-Stroke Dual-Fuel Engines use a rotating piston to compress and ignite the air-fuel mixture, providing a more compact and lightweight solution for marine applications. These engines offer the flexibility to switch between different fuels, enhancing fuel efficiency and reducing emissions.</p></p>
