<p><p>Fat-reduced cocoa powder in the market comes in two main types: natural and alkalized. Natural cocoa powder is produced by pressing cocoa beans to remove cocoa butter, resulting in a light brown powder with a slightly acidic taste. Alkalized cocoa powder, on the other hand, undergoes a process called Dutching which neutralizes the acidity and darkens the color, resulting in a milder flavor. Both types of cocoa powder are commonly used in baking, cooking, and beverages to add a rich chocolate flavor.</p></p>
