<p><p>Conductive textiles refer to fabrics that possess conductive properties, allowing them to transmit electrical signals. The market for these textiles can be categorized into three main types: woven textiles, non-woven textiles, and knitted textiles. Woven textiles are produced by interlacing two sets of yarns perpendicularly, creating a strong and durable fabric. Non-woven textiles are made by bonding fibers together, resulting in a versatile and cost-effective fabric. Knitted textiles are created by interlocking loops of yarn, providing flexibility and elasticity to the fabric. Each type of textile offers unique properties and applications within the conductive textiles market.</p></p>
