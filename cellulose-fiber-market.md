<p><p>Cellulose fiber market consists of two main types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers are derived from plants such as cotton, flax, and hemp, and are known for their biodegradability and sustainability. Man-made cellulose fibers, on the other hand, are produced through chemical processes using wood pulp or other plant-based materials. Examples of man-made cellulose fibers include viscose, modal, and lyocell. Both types of cellulose fibers are widely used in the textile industry for producing clothing, home textiles, and other products.</p></p>
