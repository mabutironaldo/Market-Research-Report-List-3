<p><p>Conventional seeds are non-GMO seeds that have been traditionally used for planting fruits and vegetables. These seeds have been saved over generations and are open-pollinated. Hybrid seeds, on the other hand, are the result of cross-breeding two different varieties of plants to produce offspring with desired traits such as disease resistance or higher yield. Hybrid seeds are often used by commercial farmers for their uniformity and productivity. Both types of seeds are widely used in the organic fruit and vegetable market to grow a variety of produce.</p></p>
