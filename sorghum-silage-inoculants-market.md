<p><p>Sorghum silage inoculants can be classified into two main types based on their fermentation process: heterofermentative and homofermentative. Heterofermentative inoculants produce a variety of fermentation products, including lactic acid, ethanol, and acetic acid, which can improve nutrient digestibility in livestock. On the other hand, homofermentative inoculants primarily produce lactic acid, leading to faster and more efficient fermentation of sorghum silage. Both types of inoculants can enhance the quality and shelf life of sorghum silage, providing benefits to farmers and livestock producers.</p></p>
