<p><ul><li>Alkali-Halide Crystals</li><li>Oxyde-Based Crystals</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inorganic scintillators can be classified into different types based on their composition. Alkali-halide crystals, such as sodium iodide and cesium iodide, are commonly used in radiation detection due to their high light output and energy resolution. Oxyde-based crystals, like cerium-doped lutetium oxyorthosilicate, offer improved performance in terms of efficiency and stability. Other types of inorganic scintillators include scintillating glasses and plastics, which offer unique properties for specific applications in radiation detection and medical imaging.</p></p>
