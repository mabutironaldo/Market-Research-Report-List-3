<p><p>Polyvinyl alcohol fibers are classified into two main types based on their structure and production process. Filament fibers are continuous strands of PVA material that are used in applications requiring high strength and durability, such as ropes and industrial fabrics. Staple fibers, on the other hand, are shorter lengths of PVA material that are spun into yarns for use in textiles and clothing. Each type of fiber has its own unique set of characteristics and applications within the market.</p></p>
