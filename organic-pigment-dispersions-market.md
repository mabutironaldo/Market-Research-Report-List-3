<p><p>Organic pigment dispersions come in two main types: water-based and oil-based. Water-based dispersions are known for their eco-friendly properties, as they are made with water as the primary solvent. They are also easier to clean up and less harmful to the environment. Oil-based dispersions, on the other hand, are preferred for their durability and resistance to water and chemicals. They are commonly used in applications where a strong bond and long-lasting color are required. Both types offer different benefits and are used in various industries.</p></p>
